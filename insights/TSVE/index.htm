Touch Sense in Virtual Environment

Cristian Silva

csprofile.github.io

Abstract

Currently all the market efforts are converging towards the development of products that create virtual reality by observing, as a form of user immersion in that environment. However, the current development focus is on exploring only the sense of sight, such as the Rift, Google Glasses and Cardboard.

The purpose of this work is to expand the virtual environment to provide the user the feeling of touch integrated to vision, making the user experience even closer to reality.

Proposal

Knowing that the human skin is provided with nervous terminations capable of capturing thermal and mechanical stimuli and those can be triggered by touch, this proposal is based on developing a device that simulates the feeling of physical user interaction in virtual environment.

The idea is to develop a glove controlled through a software, which can limit movement of each user's fingers as their interaction with a virtual object, giving the feeling of touch, hold, lift and move the object.

This gloves will consist of a series of pulleys attached to the wrist, connected to thin wires that go through the entire length of each finger and will be guided by dowels inserted between the joints as shown in Figure 1. Each of the pulleys have an independent movement, allowing restrict the movements according to the structure of the object being touched, apart of its format, by partial release, total release or cable extension block, reflecting the action to fingers and creating the sensation of touch , may even simulate different textures, as shown in Table 1.

The glove should be made of comfortable fabric, and allowing the free movement of each joints of the fingers, however, the material must be sturdy enough to carry the force applied by pulleys in the smugglers and prevent them from moving.

To simulate the feel of resistance, it is used two solenoid valves which are arranged on each side of the wrist (Figure 3), which have the function of applying a counterforce to hand movement, creating an retreat action that causes the user to have impression that reached something.

The technology proposed in this project can be used together with components of augmented reality, immersive ambiance, movement mappers, interaction with holograms, among others.

The initial idea is to create software with open source, and a simple and attractive API to developers, and provide the device to the end user with low cost and high quality.

Therefore, this device will be a breakthrough for the technology landscape that is converging to make the increasingly realistic virtual environment.
